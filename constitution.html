Mt. Sinai Alumni Group Constitution

Guiding principles and regulations for our welfare association

Preamble

We, the members of Mt. Sinai Alumni Group, recognizing the need for mutual support and collective welfare, hereby establish this constitution to guide our association. Our aim is to foster unity, provide assistance during times of need, and promote the well-being of all members and their immediate families.

Article I: Name and Objectives

Section 1: Name

1.1 The organization shall be known as the "Mt. Sinai Alumni Group Welfare Association".

Section 2: Objectives

2.1 To provide financial and moral support to members during times of need, including but not limited to funerals, sickness, educational challenges, and other emergencies.
2.2 To promote unity, cooperation, and mutual understanding among members.
2.3 To establish a sustainable welfare fund through regular contributions from members.

Article II: Membership

Section 1: Eligibility

1.1 Membership is open to all alumni of Mt. Sinai who agree to abide by this constitution.

Section 2: Registration

2.1 New members shall pay a registration fee of Ksh. 500 upon joining the welfare association.
2.2 All members must complete a registration form providing necessary personal details.
2.3 The membership list shall be updated annually in January.

Section 3: Rights and Privileges

3.1 Each member has the right to participate in all association activities.
3.2 Members are entitled to welfare benefits as outlined in this constitution.
3.3 Each member has one vote in association decisions.

Article III: Contributions

Section 1: Monthly Contributions

1.1 Each member shall contribute Ksh. 200 every month to the welfare fund.
1.2 Monthly contributions should be sent to the Treasurer on or before the 15th of every month.

Section 2: Special Contributions

2.1 Special contributions may be called for specific emergencies as determined by the officials.
2.2 The amount for special contributions shall be determined based on the nature of the emergency.

Article IV: Welfare Benefits

Section 1: Funeral Support

1.1 In the event of the death of a member or their spouse, each member will contribute Ksh. 400 for funeral expenses.
1.2 In the event of the death of a member's parent or child, each member will contribute Ksh. 300 for funeral expenses.
1.3 Members scheduled to attend funerals must do so promptly; failure may result in a fine of Ksh. 200.

Section 2: Sickness Support

2.1 Each member will contribute Ksh. 200 when a member or their immediate family (spouse or children) requires medical assistance.
2.2 The welfare will cater for hospital bills of Ksh. 10,000 and above, after assessment by officials.
2.3 For bills below the total expected contribution (200 × number of members), the amount will be divided equally among members.

Section 3: Other Emergencies

3.1 Support for educational difficulties and other intense emergencies shall be determined case by case by the officials.
3.2 Withdrawals for support will only be made once at least three officials have agreed on the assistance to be extended.

Article V: Governance

Section 1: Officials

1.1 The association shall have a committee of officials including a Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer.
1.2 Three officials shall be signatories to the association's bank account.
1.3 Officials shall be elected annually by members.

Section 2: Meetings

2.1 Meetings shall be called through SMS or other agreed communication channels.
2.2 The Secretary or organizer shall take roll calls during meetings.
2.3 A member who fails to attend two consecutive meetings without valid reason may have their membership dismissed.

Section 3: Financial Management

3.1 A monthly audit of funds shall be conducted to ensure transparency.
3.2 Financial records shall be available for inspection by members upon request.

Article VI: Discipline

Section 1: Misconduct

1.1 Any misconduct by a member during meetings will result in a warning for the first offense.
1.2 A second offense of misconduct may lead to termination of membership.

Section 2: Contribution Defaulters

2.1 If a member defaults on contributions, the matter will be discussed by officials.
2.2 The committee may terminate membership or apply penalties for consistent defaulting.
2.3 Faking documents to receive benefits will result in immediate termination.

Section 3: Adherence

3.1 Members who refuse to adhere to this constitution will be excluded from the welfare.
3.2 The association will move forward with serious and committed members only.

Article VII: Amendments

Section 1: Process

1.1 This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of members present in a specially convened meeting.
1.2 Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the Secretary at least two weeks before the meeting.

Article VIII: Dissolution

Section 1: Process

1.1 The association may be dissolved by a three-quarters majority vote of all members.
1.2 Upon dissolution, any remaining funds after settling liabilities shall be distributed equally among current members.

Adoption

This constitution was adopted by the members of Mt. Sinai Alumni Group Welfare Association on [Date of Adoption].

Together we grow, together we stand.
